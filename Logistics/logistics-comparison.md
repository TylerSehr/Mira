# Logistics Options: Side-by-Side Comparison

**Product:** Smart glasses + charging case (Li-ion battery) + smart ring
**Declared value:** \$500 | **Weight:** 0.2 kg | **Origin:** Shenzhen | **Destination:** US (DTC ecommerce)
**Duties:** ~\$52-\$190/unit depending on HS code classification (see Tariff Uncertainty section below)

---

## Tariff Uncertainty (Critical)

The duty estimate used in this report is uncertain because the Mira product bundle contains three components that classify under **different HS codes**, each with different duty rates:

| Product | Likely HS Code | Heading | MFN Base | Section 301 | Notes |
|---------|---------------|---------|----------|-------------|-------|
| Mira Glasses | 8517.62 | Communication apparatus | 0-2% | 7.5% (List 4A) | CBP consistently classifies smart glasses with Bluetooth/AI here, not as eyewear (9004) |
| MiraCase (charging case) | 8504.40 | Static converters | 1.5-3.3% | 7.5-25% (verify list) | CBP classifies charging cases/power banks as static converters, NOT accessories |
| Mira Ring | 9031.80 or 8517.62 | Instruments or comm. apparatus | 0-2.6% | 7.5% (verify list) | Depends on whether ring is primarily a sensor or a Bluetooth input device |

**Section 122 surcharge (10%)** applies to all -- but expires July 24, 2026 (150-day statutory limit) unless Congress extends it.

## The Six Options

| Option | Description | Partners Involved |

| **C** | FF + US 3PL | Freight forwarder + US 3PL (ShipMonk or similar) |
| **D** | Self-Managed SZ + FF DDP | Your own SZ warehouse/staff + freight forwarder |

| **F** | Self-Managed QC + Flexport | Your SZ QC hire + Flexport (freight + US fulfillment) |

---

## How Each Option Works

### Option A: FF Direct DDP
Factory boxes product -> freight forwarder flies it DDP to customer's door. Nobody inspects anything.

### Option B: SZ 3PL + FF DDP
Factory ships to SZ 3PL -> 3PL inspects, packs, hands to freight forwarder -> FF ships DDP to customer. QC happens, but delivery is still 7-15 days from China.

### Option C: FF + US 3PL
Factory ships to freight forwarder -> FF bulk ships to US 3PL warehouse -> US 3PL fulfills orders domestically (2-5 days). No QC step.

### Option D: Self-Managed SZ + FF DDP
Factory ships to your own SZ warehouse -> your staff inspects, packs -> freight forwarder ships DDP to customer. Full control, high overhead. Requires WFOE registration.

### Option E: SZ 3PL + FF + US 3PL (Hybrid)
Factory ships to SZ 3PL -> 3PL inspects, repacks into bulk cartons -> freight forwarder bulk ships to US 3PL -> US 3PL fulfills domestically. Best of both worlds: QC + fast delivery.

### Option F: Self-Managed QC + Flexport (Hybrid)
Factory ships to your QC person in Shenzhen -> they inspect, repack -> Flexport picks up, handles freight + customs + US warehousing + fulfillment. One logistics platform, you control QC.

## Summary: Which Option When?

| Situation | Best Option | Why |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Pre-launch, testing market fit, <100 orders/mo | **A** | Zero cost, zero complexity, zero risk |
| Growing, QC matters, not ready for US inventory | **B** | Adds QC at minimal cost, still no capital required |
| 500+ orders/mo, have \$95K+ capital, want fast delivery | **E** | QC + fast delivery + returns -- the full package |
| Same as above, want simplest ops with one platform | **F** | Flexport handles freight+fulfillment, you just do QC |
| High volume (2,000+/mo), optimizing for lowest cost | **C or E** | Bulk efficiency; add E if QC is important |
| Need proprietary QC/customization that 3PLs can't do | **D** | Only if you truly need hands-on control in SZ |

## Bottom Line

**All six options cost roughly the same per order** at 500/month. Whether duties end up at \$90 or \$190, the shipping+handling difference between options is only \$4-\$12. Duties dominate the total cost, making the option choice nearly irrelevant on price alone.

**The decision comes down to three questions:**

1. **Do you have \$95K+ in working capital?**
   - No -> A or B (ship per order, no inventory)
   - Yes -> C, E, or F (pre-stock US inventory)

2. **Does QC matter for your product?**
   - Yes (it should, at \$500) -> B, D, E, or F
   - Willing to risk it -> A or C

3. **How many relationships do you want to manage?**
   - One -> A (simplest) or F (Flexport handles most)
   - Two -> B or C
   - Three -> E (most capability, most coordination)

**Recommended path:**
- **Launch:** Option A (validate market, \$0 upfront)
- **Growth:** Option B (add QC, still no capital needed)
- **Scale:** Option E or F (QC + US fulfillment + fast delivery)
