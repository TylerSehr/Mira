## DEVICE 1: MIRA GLASSES

---

### 1. FORM FACTOR & MECHANICAL

**Overall form:** Full-rim eyeglass frame in a conventional spectacles form factor. Designed to be worn all-day as a standard pair of glasses with embedded electronics.

**Frame material:** Not officially disclosed. For this weight class (39g total) with integrated optics, battery, and electronics, the frame is likely [INFERRED] a combination of:
- Injection-molded nylon or TR-90 (thermoplastic rubber) for the front frame — lightweight, flexible, hypoallergenic
- Metal alloy (titanium or stainless steel) hinges for durability
- Silicone or acetate nose pads

**Weight:** 39 grams total assembled weight
- Comparable: standard plastic eyeglasses = ~20–25g; Meta Ray-Ban Display = ~70g
- Mira achieves roughly half the weight of Meta's display glasses despite similar feature set

**Frame width:** 145.6mm (5.73 inches)
- Standard adult medium-to-large fit

**Temples (arms):** Electronics are housed in thickened temple arms — this is standard packaging for all smart glasses. The temples would contain:
- Battery cell(s)
- PCB / SoC
- Microphone(s)
- Speaker driver(s)
- Bluetooth radio
- Charging contacts or port

**Prescription compatibility:** Yes. Custom lenses available using 1.74 high-index optics — the thinnest and lightest lens index available commercially. These are ground to prescription and installed in the frame. Non-refundable / non-reworkable once ordered.

### 2. DISPLAY SYSTEM

**Type:** Dual waveguide display
- One waveguide per lens (binocular configuration)
- Each waveguide is a thin optical element embedded in or laminated to the lens that redirects light from a projector into the wearer's eye

**Visibility:** Fully private. The waveguide redirects light only toward the wearer's eye at a specific angle — observers looking at the glasses from the outside cannot see the projected image.

**Display category:** "Display glasses" — sits between:
- Voice-only AI glasses (no display, e.g. Ray-Ban Meta Gen 2): audio output only
- Full AR glasses (e.g. XREAL Air): spatially-anchored overlays, 6DoF tracking
- Mira occupies the middle tier: screen-on-lens for text/UI, no spatial AR

**Content rendered:** Text responses, notifications, translations, teleprompter text, AI prompts. Not a full spatial AR environment.

**Projector type:** Not officially disclosed. For dual waveguide systems at this weight and power budget, the projector is likely [INFERRED] one of:
- Micro-OLED (most common in premium display glasses — high brightness, compact)
- LCoS (Liquid Crystal on Silicon) — used in some waveguide systems
- Micro-LED (emerging, very power efficient)
The projector sits at the edge of the lens and couples light into the waveguide.

**Waveguide construction:** Not officially disclosed. Likely [INFERRED]:
- Geometric/diffractive waveguide (similar to Lumus-style reflective waveguide or WaveOptics diffractive)
- Thin glass or plastic substrate
- Coatings optimized for light coupling efficiency

**Field of view:** Not disclosed. Display glasses in this class typically offer [INFERRED] a limited, central FoV (approximately 20–40°) sufficient for text readout, not wide-angle spatial AR.

**Resolution:** Not disclosed publicly.

**Color:** Press describes it as "color screen" — not monochrome (monochrome waveguides are cheaper/simpler; color indicates a more capable projector/waveguide stack).

### 3. AUDIO SYSTEM

**Microphones:**
- Always-on configuration
- Quantity not disclosed; smart glasses in this class typically use [INFERRED] 2–4 MEMS microphones for:
  - Beamforming (directional audio pickup toward wearer's voice)
  - Noise cancellation (filtering ambient audio from speech)
  - Wind noise reduction
- Positioned in the temple arms, near the wearer's ears and mouth

**Speakers:**
- Built-in, described as "crystal-clear audio"
- Type: likely [INFERRED] open-ear speakers (directional drivers angled toward the ear canal without insertion) — same approach as Ray-Ban Meta, Bose Frames, etc.
- Positioned in temple arms near the ear

**Audio processing pipeline:**
1. Microphone captures raw audio continuously
2. On-device or near-device pre-processing (noise reduction, VAD — voice activity detection)
3. Audio streamed via Bluetooth to paired iPhone
4. iPhone app runs speech-to-text transcription
5. Transcript is stored locally; raw audio is immediately and permanently deleted
6. AI model processes transcript for responses and proactive insights
7. Response text sent back to glasses via Bluetooth; displayed on waveguide

**No camera:** Explicitly absent by design. No image sensor, no image signal processor (ISP), no optics for imaging. This simplifies the BOM, reduces power consumption, and removes regulatory/privacy surface area.

### 4. COMPUTE & CONNECTIVITY

**On-device processor:** Not disclosed. Given the weight and battery life targets, the glasses themselves likely run a minimal embedded MCU or DSP [INFERRED] responsible for:
- Audio capture and pre-processing
- Bluetooth stack management
- Display driver control
- Power management
- Firmware over-the-air (OTA) update support (confirmed via App Store version history showing firmware updates)

**Primary compute:** Offloaded to the paired iPhone via Bluetooth. The iPhone runs the Mira app, which handles:
- Full speech-to-text transcription
- AI model API calls (Google / Anthropic backends)
- Transcript storage
- Response rendering and push to glasses display

**Connectivity:** Bluetooth (version not specified; likely [INFERRED] BT 5.x for low-energy, low-latency audio streaming)

**Wireless charging / port:** Not specified. Smart glasses in this class typically use [INFERRED] a magnetic charging cradle or USB-C port in the temple.

**AI response latency:** Sub-700ms end-to-end (microphone capture → transcription → AI inference → display render). This is the full round-trip including cloud API calls to Google/Anthropic.

### 5. POWER SYSTEM

**Battery life:** 10–12+ hours claimed (all-day wear target)
**Battery type:** Lithium-ion or lithium-polymer cells [INFERRED], distributed in the temple arms
**Battery capacity:** Not disclosed. For reference, Meta Ray-Ban Display (70g, ~8hr) uses a similar split-cell architecture; Mira's lighter weight (39g) with longer claimed life suggests either:
- More efficient display/projector system
- Lower-power SoC
- Aggressive power gating when display is not active
**Charging:** Method not specified

### 6. OPTICAL LENSES

**Standard version:** Clear optic/sunglass lens with waveguide embedded or laminated
**Prescription version:** Custom 1.74 high-index lenses
- 1.74 is the highest index available for plastic lenses (thinnest and lightest at high prescriptions)
- Ground to wearer's Rx and fitted in the frame
- Non-refundable / non-reworkable once ordered — indicates custom manufacturing, not off-the-shelf

## KEY HARDWARE DESIGN DECISIONS & TRADEOFFS

| Decision | Tradeoff |
|----------|----------|
| No camera | Faster AI pipeline; better privacy; simpler regulatory path; BUT limits future use cases |
| Dual waveguide (not monocular) | Binocular display; more natural; BUT higher BOM cost and weight vs. single waveguide |
| Audio-first, compute offloaded to phone | Keeps glasses light and battery life long; BUT requires constant Bluetooth connection to iPhone |
| 1.74 high-index prescription lenses | Thinnest/lightest possible Rx lenses; BUT increases unit cost and complicates manufacturing (custom grind) |
| 39g target weight | Below perception threshold for discomfort in all-day wear; BUT constrains battery capacity and display brightness |
| 10–12hr battery | All-day single-charge use; differentiates from Meta Ray-Ban (~8hr); achievement at 39g suggests power-efficient display |
| Ring controller (separate device) | Enables discreet input without touching glasses; BUT adds a second device to charge/manage/lose |

## NOTES FOR MANUFACTURING / ENGINEERING

- Mira is actively seeking a Founding Hardware Operations Lead to build out supply chain and move from prototype to production — suggesting manufacturing processes are not yet fully stabilized
- The waveguide display is the most technically complex and cost-critical subsystem; waveguide manufacturers of note include Lumus, WaveOptics (acquired by Snap), and DigiLens
- Frame fabrication for smart glasses at this weight class likely requires Chinese OEM/ODM partners (Shenzhen for smart electronics integration; Danyang for optical lens grinding)
- Prescription lens fulfillment adds a custom-manufacturing step that complicates standard consumer electronics fulfillment pipelines
- OTA firmware updates are confirmed (App Store changelog references firmware update versions) — requires secure bootloader and BT DFU capability on the glasses MCU
