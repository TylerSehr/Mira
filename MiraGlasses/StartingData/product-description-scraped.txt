## PRODUCT LINE OVERVIEW

Mira (trymira.com) makes two primary hardware products that work together as a system:
1. **Mira Glasses** — AI smart glasses with a dual waveguide display
2. **Mira Ring** — Touch-sensitive ring controller, included with glasses purchase

Both are paired with the **Mira App** (iOS), which acts as the second brain interface.

---

## PRODUCT 1: MIRA GLASSES

### What It Is
Mira Glasses are lightweight AI smart glasses designed for continuous, all-day wear. They listen to everything happening around the wearer, transcribe it in real time, and surface relevant information, answers, and proactive insights directly in the wearer's field of view via a dual waveguide display — without a camera and without touching a phone.

The device is positioned as the "cognitive copilot" — an ambient AI that lives with you rather than something you interact with reactively.

### Physical Specifications
| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Weight | 39 grams |
| Frame Width | 145.6mm (5.73 inches) |
| Battery Life | 10–12+ hours (claimed) |
| Camera | None |
| Display | Dual waveguide display (private — only wearer can see) |
| Microphone | Yes — always-on |
| Speakers | Yes — built-in, crystal-clear audio |
| Connectivity | Bluetooth (pairs with iPhone) |
| Prescription | Available — custom 1.74 index lenses (thinnest/lightest available) |

### Display Technology
- **Type:** Dual waveguide display
- **Visibility:** Fully private — only the wearer can see what's shown
- **Content:** Surfaces AI text responses, notifications, translations, prompts
- **Category:** "Display glasses" — falls between voice-only AI glasses and full AR (provides a color screen for notifications and AI responses without full spatial AR)

### Audio System
- Always-on microphones capture all conversations and ambient audio
- Built-in speakers for audio feedback
- No video/camera capability — audio only

### AI Response Performance
- Sub-700 millisecond latency for AI responses
- Claimed to be faster than many cloud-based competing services
- Designed to feel like natural thought speed

### No Camera — Intentional Design Choice
Unlike Meta Ray-Ban glasses, Mira deliberately omits a camera. This enables:
- Higher AI response speed (audio-only processing pipeline is faster)
- Stronger privacy positioning (no visual recording of others)
- Simpler regulatory profile
- The founders cite this as a core differentiator after their I-XRAY facial recognition demo demonstrated the risks of camera-equipped wearables

## PRODUCT 2: MIRA RING

### What It Is
A touch-sensitive ring controller that ships included with the Mira Glasses purchase. It serves as the primary physical input device so users never need to touch the glasses themselves.

### Features
- Touch-sensitive surface
- Gesture/tap controls for:
  - Answering calls
  - Activating the AI assistant
  - Navigating menus
  - Skipping songs
- Discreet — designed to be inconspicuous in professional settings
- Included with glasses (not sold separately, based on available info)

## PRODUCT 3: MIRA APP (iOS)

### What It Is
The Mira App (developer: Industrials, Inc.) is the companion application that acts as the brain behind the glasses. It stores transcripts, manages AI queries, surfaces proactive insights, and connects the hardware to the AI backend.

### App Store Details
- **App Name:** Mira Glasses
- **Developer:** Industrials, Inc.
- **Category:** Productivity
- **Size:** 73.1 MB
- **Compatibility:** iOS 18.0+; works on iPhone, iPad, iPod touch
- **Rating:** 4.1/5 stars (26 ratings at time of research)
- **Age Rating:** 4+

### Core Features (Free)
- **Unlimited meeting transcription** — all conversations auto-transcribed
- **Automatic note-taking** — key points extracted from meetings
- **Real-time translation** — 60+ languages, instant and automatic
- **Conversation summaries** — AI-generated summaries of discussions
- **Memory recall** — search and retrieve past conversations
- **Proactive insights** — AI surfaces relevant context without being asked
- **Teleprompter mode** — assists with presentation delivery; text scrolls in glasses display

### Premium Features ($19.99/month or $191.99/year)
- Unlimited AI assistant queries (free tier: 3 questions/day)
- Advanced proactive insights
- Early customers receive 1-month free trial of unlimited AI

### Custom Skills
- Users can configure 3 custom AI skills/behaviors tailored to their specific workflow
- Described by users as "the main game changer" — allows the AI to act as a domain-specific assistant

### AI Backend
- Powered by language models from **Google (Alphabet)** and **Anthropic**
- Custom agent framework built by Mira's internal engineering team
- Processes extensive conversation histories for personalized, proactive responses

### Data & Privacy
- Audio recordings deleted immediately after transcription (not stored anywhere)
- Transcripts stored locally on user's phone only (not on Mira's servers)
- Company never sells or trains on user data
- Cannot distinguish between different speakers (only captures wearer's perspective)

### Version History (recent)
- **v1.0.31 (March 2026):** Added real-time AI feature; fixed Bluetooth connectivity issues
- Earlier versions: transcription improvements, firmware updates, notification features in glasses

## PRODUCT PHILOSOPHY

**Core insight:** Memory is humanity's fundamental cognitive bottleneck. People forget names seconds after hearing them, lose meeting details, and waste time pulling phones out to retrieve information.

**Solution philosophy:** Rather than building a reactive assistant (ask → answer), Mira is designed as a proactive system that lives with the user and surfaces relevant information before they have to ask. "For the first time, assistance can be proactive and conversational, not reactive and procedural."

**Long-term vision:** The founders want technology that frees humans from routine cognitive tasks so they can focus on "what only humans can do, and what an AI can't." The glasses are step one toward ambient computing replacing the smartphone as the primary human-computer interface.
