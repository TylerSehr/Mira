## PRODUCT: MIRA GLASSES

### Core Concept
Mira positions itself as the "cognitive copilot" and "second brain" — AI-powered smart glasses that remember everything and provide proactive, real-time intelligence. The device is audio-first with no camera, designed for continuous daily wear.

### Mission / Vision
"We want humans to dive into what makes them so special. To spend time on what only humans can do, and what an AI can't."

The founders identify memory as humanity's fundamental cognitive bottleneck — people forget names, lose meeting details, and waste time retrieving info from phones. Mira aims to fix this via wearable ambient AI.

### Hardware Specifications
- **Weight:** 39 grams (approximately half the weight of Meta Ray-Ban glasses)
- **Width:** 145.6mm (5.73 inches)
- **Battery Life:** 10–12+ hours on a single charge (claimed as double that of competing smart glasses)
- **Display:** Dual waveguide display (dual-lens, fully private — only the wearer can see it)
- **Microphone:** Yes — always-on, continuously captures audio
- **Camera:** NO camera
- **Response Latency:** Sub-700 milliseconds (fast enough to feel like natural thought)
- **Accessories Included:** Touch-sensitive ring controller

### Ring Controller
- Touch-sensitive ring included with the glasses
- Used to answer calls, activate AI, and navigate without touching the glasses
- Discreet gesture control

### Key Features
- **Unlimited recording** — records all conversations continuously
- **Automatic audio deletion** — audio is deleted immediately after transcription; only transcripts are retained
- **Local transcript storage** — transcripts stored on user's phone, NOT on company servers
- **Meeting notes & summaries** — automatic key point extraction from meetings
- **Real-time translation** — 60+ languages, claimed as instant and automatic
- **Memory recall** — remembers names, conversations, meeting context
- **Proactive AI assistance** — surfaces relevant context without prompting
- **Search / Q&A** — direct information retrieval without a smartphone
- **Follow-up questions** — suggests relevant follow-ups
- **Calculations**

### AI Backend
- Powered by language models from Google (Alphabet) and Anthropic
- Custom agent framework built by internal engineering team
- Processes extensive conversation histories for "proactive insights"

### Privacy Model
- No camera
- Audio recordings are deleted immediately after transcription
- Transcripts stored locally on user's phone (not on Mira's servers)
- Company never sells or trains on user data
- Cannot distinguish between different speakers except the wearer
- Users expected to obtain consent before recording (consistent with wearable tech norms)

### Pricing
- **Standard:** $479
- **Discounted:** $429 (discount offered until Dec. 31 of launch year)
- **With prescription lenses:** $599 ($549 discounted)
- **Subscription:** 3 free daily AI questions included; $20/month for premium/unlimited AI access
- **Free tier includes:** Unlimited meeting transcription, note-taking, translation

### Target Users
- Executives
- Sales teams
- Engineers
- Professionals requiring hands-free intelligence during critical moments

### Launch Timeline

- Shipping: January 2026

## COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

**Primary Competitor:** Meta Ray-Ban AI Glasses

**Mira's differentiation:**
- No camera (privacy-first approach)
- Lighter than Meta Ray-Bans
- Longer battery life than Meta Ray-Bans
- Audio-only transcription enables faster response times
- Local data storage vs. cloud dependency
- Proactive "second brain" vs. reactive "Siri-style assistant"
- "Hyper-intelligent second brain to remember everything and get daily proactive insights" vs. Meta's "assistant to answer basic questions"

**Other context:**
- Positioned against Ray-Ban Meta AI Glasses, with continuous listening rather than only responding to direct queries
- Industry observers predict smartphones may eventually be replaced by advanced eyewear in the AI era
